Today’s AI can’t be trusted
['After years of relatively low-profile progress, Artificial Intelligence is now on everyone’s mind. Anyone following the news is familiar with the spectacular debut of', '—the fastest growing consumer application in history—and billions of people have first-hand experience with AI through Siri, Alexa, and numerous online services. Supercharged worldwide enthusiasm has concurrently led to concern from governments about potential risks from AI technology, like labor disruption, manipulated or deceptive content, appropriation of private data, and even possible existential threats from imagined superintelligent systems. In a sign of the level of concern, the recent UK AI Safety Summit produced the „Bletchley Declaration“, expressing the intent of the international community to work together toward “human-centric” and responsible AI, especially at what they call the „‘frontier’ of AI“.']
